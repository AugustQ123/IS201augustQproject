My Top 5 Ski Resorts in Utah

  • Sundance
  • Brighton
  • Snowbird
  • Solitude
  • Deer Valley

Sundance

Sundance is the resort that I have spent the most time at. I always get the season pass for Sundance. It's only 20 minutes away from BYU and it has everything I need. It is however not the biggest resort, but this also means that it is never too crowded.

Brighton

Brighton is known as being the bro-resort and for a good reason. This place has a ton of good tree-trails, very nice powder areas and some sick drop offs over cliffs. It is however always very crowded and the culture here is a bit judgmental at the park lol.

Snowbird

This is what I consider a very nice resort. It is very big and has ton of different fun trails to take. A lot of what makes Brighton good, also makes Snowbird good. You can tell that the crew working here put pride in their work and the resort is always up to date.

Solitude

The reason why I love Solitude and why so many others do is because of the difficulty level here. Solitude is in the top 10% hardest resorts in the country. The blacks here are complicated and will give you a challenge. This also eliminates too many newcomers on the mountain, so you don't get stuck behind anyone.

Deer Valley

I had to include Deer Valley as a resort because it is just in a class of its own. I have been there a few times on skis, because Deer Valley does not allow Snowboarders on the mountain. I do however recommend that everyone tries this resort at least once as it has some high quality riding.
